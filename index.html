Hi! I'm Helen, and I'm a second-year PhD student in Computational Neuroscience and Psychology. While my research primarily focuses on understanding how humans process narratives—such as stories and movies—using neuroimaging, I have a longstanding interest in Mathematics, Statistics, Data Science, and Machine Learning. I find it especially exciting to experiment with and apply both supervised and unsupervised learning methods to high-dimensional data. In the past, I have worked extensively with cutting-edge large language models, deep learning architectures, and hidden Markov models, using both Python and C++. In my free time, I enjoy skiing and watching movies.
