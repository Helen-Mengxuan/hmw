Hi! I'm Helen, and I'm a 3rd-year PhD student working in Computational Neuroscience.

My research primarily focuses on (1) understanding how the human brain and large language models process continuous streams of information, and (2) developing encoding and decoding methods for neural activity. I primarily work with neuroimaging data and have over three years of experience working with magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) of the brain.

Here is my recent publication on using Hidden Markov Models to decode human brain activity related to event parsing while participants listened to two auditory stories. In the past, I have also worked on decoding human emotional states from fMRI data collected while participants watched two short animations designed to evoke mixed feelings.

Recently, I have been investigating (1) which brain regions produce neural embeddings most similar to LLM embeddings during information processing, and (2) the dissociation between regions responsible for theory of mind and autobiographical memory within the medial default mode network. The default mode network (DMN) is a set of interconnected brain regions that are most active during internally focused processes such as self-referential thinking, imagining others’ mental states, and processing continuous information.

Outside the field of neuroscience, I have a longstanding interest in mathematics, statistics, data science, and machine learning. When I’m not experimenting with supervised or unsupervised learning methods to analyze high-dimensional datasets, you can find me skiing or watching movies!
